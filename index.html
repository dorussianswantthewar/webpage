DO RUSSIANS
WANT THE WAR?
DNIPRO, UKRAINE
JANUARY 14, 2023
WHO IS
THIS FOR
“Let’s ask the soldiers who now lie beneath the birch trees and the sky,
and let their sons tell you once more whether the Russians long for war.”

- a 1961 soviet anti-war song

     In February 2022 Russia has started an invasion of Ukraine. While the Russian army has suffered (and continues to suffer) heavy losses, their civilians are safe at home unlike the Ukrainian people — there were far more civilian casualties than those of the military personnel. Tens of thousands of people have died, many more were injured or lost their loved ones, their homes. Peaceful cities, towns and villages were damaged to the point of being inhabitable.


     The Russian propaganda treats this war like some kind of an “inner conflict” between two post-USSR countries, which implies
that no other countries will suffer from Putin’s invasion in the future. But it’s a lie, and it should be obvious after you’ve seen what Russians themselves have to say. That’s what this website was created for.


    Here we’ve collected and translated to English multiple quotes from Russian government officials and regular people to show
that there’s no hope for a diplomatic solution in this war sadly. If Russians would’ve wanted to stop the invasion — they could’ve
done it by withdrawing their troops months ago, and they still can do this today. But they won’t.


    Why? Well, let’s just ask them.
